2.1 Gender¶

Males contributed 57597 (57.41%) happy moments to the corpus, and females contributed 42019 (41.88%) moments. The authors of 697 (0.69%) moments were not willing to disclose their genders.

Each sentence is predicted to belong to one of the following categories: affection, achievement, bonding, enjoy the moment, exercise, leisure, and nature. A cross tabulation of gender and predicted category showed that females disproportionally contributed to moments in affection (50.37%) and nature (48.23%). Even though females' proportion in the author population is only 41.88%, they contributed to about half of the happy moments related to affection and nature, which reveals females' tendency to value intimate interpersonal relationships more than males, and suggests that females are more likely to appreciate happiness related to nature.

On the other hand, males have disproportionally high contribution to moments in exercise (68.62%), leisure (64.76%), and achievement (62.31%), which goes with the stereotype that males are more ambitious and active. Males are also slightly more likely to mention moments in bonding (60.23%) and "enjoy-the-moment" (60.38%) kind of happiness than females, although not by a great amount.

I did not exclude those who did not disclose the gender from the analysis, because these people might have special characteristics, such as bisexual identification. This group has disproportionally high contribution to moments in enjoy-the-moment (0.86%), exercise (0.84%), and bonding (0.82%).

2.3 What kind of happiness lasts and what fades away¶

Category: As mentioned earlier, each sentence is assigned to one of the categories by a prediction algorithm. Let's look at the popularity of each category by plotting a bargraph of all the predicted categories. (Note: each sentence belongs to a category predicted by an algorithm, but only 14107 out of the 100392 moments are manually labelled).

The bargraph showed that the two most popular categories are affection and achievement, followed by enjoy-the-moment, bonding, and leisure.

Reflection period: Each sentence has an associated reflection period of 24 hours or 3 months, and the author can only recall happy moments occurring within the specified time frame. There are 50646 moments recalled within 3 months, and 49746 recalled within the last 24 hours. Here we analyze what categories of moments are more likely to bring people happiness in the long run versus the short run.

The bargraph "Predicted Category and Reflection Period" showed that more moments in achievement and affection are recalled in the long run than the short run, meaning that these moments are more salient in people's memory in the long run, compared to other categories.

On the other hand, enjoy-the-moment and leisure moments are more likely to be remembered within 24 hours, which makes sense, because these happy moments are usually more trivial (e.g., watched a good movie, had a good meal, etc.) than moments of achievement (e.g., got promoted, got good grade) and affection (e.g., strong emotion, connection).

Part 5: K-Means Clustering¶

To validate the result from LDA, I also applied K-means clustering to cluster the documents. The topic words are pretty similar to that in LDA. From those topic words, we can see that cluster 0 is about playing games (maybe with friends), cluster 1 is about job, cluster 2 and 3 are about son and daughter, respectively, and cluster 4 are about friend and events.

I also used PCA to visualize the word vectors in the 2-D space. We can see that the orange, red, and blue groups are somewhat overlapped with each other, and indeed their semantic meaning are also close (son, daughter, home, etc.). The other two categories are more separate from the rest of the groups.
